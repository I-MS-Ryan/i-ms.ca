IMS is live — and here's what's coming first

After years of working in building code administration and municipal inspections, the gaps in available software became impossible to ignore. IMS exists to fill those gaps — starting with the apps that solve the most common, most urgent problems: building permits, fire inspections, and asset management.

We're building in order of impact. The first apps to ship are focused on the permitting and inspection workflows that every municipality runs, whether they have two staff or two hundred.

What we're building in IMS-Fire — and why it's different

The fire inspection software market is dominated by enterprise tools priced for large departments. Most Saskatchewan volunteer fire departments are doing inspections on paper. IMS-Fire is being built for them — occupancy-based checklists, offline pre-incident plans, and owner-side deficiency reporting that doesn't require installing an app.

Most municipal software on the market was designed for American jurisdictions or large Canadian cities. The NBC, SK Fire Code, provincial asset management requirements, and the reality of rural and small-town operations rarely drive the product roadmap. At IMS, they're the starting point.

Rural asset management: what RMs actually need to track and report

Saskatchewan rural municipalities face a specific challenge: large geographic areas, limited staff, and growing provincial reporting obligations for infrastructure condition and capital planning. IMS-Asset Rural is being designed around those minimum requirements — simple enough for a two-person office, complete enough to satisfy the province.
